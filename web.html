Roystonea regia

Roystonea regia, commonly known as the Cuban royal palm or Florida royal palm.

Uses :-

Roystonea regia has been planted throughout the tropics and subtropics as an ornamental.[19] The seed is used as a source of oil and for livestock feed. Leaves are used for thatching and the wood for construction.[7] The roots are used as a diuretic, [37] and for that reason they are added to tifey, a Haitian drink, by Cubans of Haitian origin.[38] They are also used as a treatment for diabetes
